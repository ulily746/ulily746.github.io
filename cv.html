Summary

Master’s student in Chemical and Biomolecular Engineering with research experience in PLGA-PEG nanoparticle-based drug delivery systems for dual metabolic inhibition in cancer therapy. Skilled in nanoparticle characterization (DLS, TEM, HPLC), and therapeutic evaluation in cellular and in vivo models. Building on this experimental foundation, I aim to advance toward data-driven and computationally integrated smart drug delivery systems, with the goal of enabling more precise diagnosis and intelligent therapeutic strategies.

Research Experience

Graduate Student Researcher – KAIST

2023 – 2025

- Designed and optimized PLGA-PEG nanoparticles for dual metabolic inhibition in hepatocellular carcinoma (HepG2) by encapsulating shikonin (glycolysis inhibitor) and atovaquone (OXPHOS inhibitor). - Conducted in vitro assays (cell viability, apoptosis, lactate/glucose metabolism, ROS/ATP/JC-1/MitoSOX, GSH) to evaluate therapeutic efficacy and mechanisms in cancer vs. normal cells. - Performed nanoparticle characterization using DLS, HPLC, TEM, and CLSM; investigated biodistribution and therapeutic efficacy in orthotopic liver tumor models. - Analyzed data using Prism, FlowJo, CompuSyn, and ZEN.

Field Training Intern – Pusan National University

2020 – 2021

- Developed membrane-based exosome isolation system; fabricated and characterized membranes using SEM and DLS. - Operated a tangential flow filtration system, including design and setup of custom modules for exosome separation. -Conducted exosome characterization with TEM and DLS; collaborated on device prototyping with acid/base-assisted fabrication and 3D modeling.
